Copyright Design Patents Act

The CDPA protects creators and owners of of intellectual properties and original works from unauthorised copying adapting and distrubution

Copyright

The CDPA allows an individual that has created an original piece of work the right to control the use of their work. The Act includes digital works and the legislation covers:

  • Literary works
  • Dramatic works
  • Musical works
  • Typographical works
  • Sound recordings
  • Films
  • Computer programs

Patents

A patent allows an original idea within certain fields to be protected. The shape or configuration of a product or item is protected by design rights.

Trademarks

A trademark protects indications of the commercial source of a product or service like a logo and remain in force as long as they are actively used or registered.

Patent law controversies

Widely considered the most abusable form of protection is patents due to how broad the coverage of patents can be leading to patent trolls and law suits that can damage the tech industry and prevent advancement for example:

  • VirnetX sued apple for $454 million for breaching its patents on secure network technology with Facetime and VPNs
  • NTP Inc sued RIM for $625 million for infringing on patents on wireless email
However those cases were in the USA patent law is stricter in the UK but patents are still put in to question for example:

  • Apple is being sued by optis for infringing on their patents on 4G technology in their iphones and other devices and were charged to pay $56.43 million dollars plus interest which was then appealed and determined that a royalty of $0.15 per unit was appropriate which summed to $502 million plus interest the case has not yet been settled but still shows the potential for tech patent weaponization in the UK
