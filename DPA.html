Data Protection Act 2018

The DPA 2018 builds on the DPA 1998 and the EU's GDPR

The data protection act was created to ensure ethical data usage and storage aswell as providing you specific rights over your data for your protection and privacy.

Roles

  • In terms of your data being stored you would be the Data Subject
  • The person in control of the data collection, usage and storage from the data subjects is the Data Controller
  • The Information Commissioner's Office is the public body that enforces the data protection act

Data subject's rights

As the data subject you have the right to:

  • access your data
  • correct your data
  • prevent distress
  • prevent direct marketing
  • prevent automated decisions
  • complain to the information comissioner
  • compensation

Data Protection Principles

Data must:

  • only be held for specific reasons
  • be collected and used fairly and inside the law
  • only be used for the registered purpose it was intended
  • be kept accurate and up to date
  • be kept safe and secure
  • be adequate relevant not excessive
  • not be kept longer than necessary
  • not be transfered outside DPA/GDPR jurisdiction

Personal and sensitiive data

There are stricter regulations for sensitive data

Personal Data is data used to identify someone for example:

  • Full name
  • Address
  • Phone number
  • Date of birth
  • IP address
  • Banking details
  • financial transactions

Sensitive Data is very private protected characteristics that could cause harm if exposed for example:

  • Nationality
  • Ethnicity
  • Political opinions
  • Trade union membership
  • Genetics
  • Biometrics
  • Health
